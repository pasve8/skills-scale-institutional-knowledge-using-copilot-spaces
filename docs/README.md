# OctoAcme Project Management Process Documentation

This folder is the canonical entry point for OctoAcme project management documentation. The process is designed to move work from idea to release through a lightweight, repeatable lifecycle: initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective improvement. Teams start with a project one-pager, success metrics, initial risks, and stakeholder alignment before work is approved for planning.

From there, planning turns approved goals into a prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria, estimates, dependencies, and a shared Definition of Done. During execution, teams use a visible board workflow (Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, Done), maintain regular standups and weekly syncs, and track delivery metrics and blockers. Risk management and escalation paths are embedded in normal delivery rhythm, with clear ownership from team-level triage through PM/Product Lead and sponsor escalation when needed.

Roles and personas are intentionally cross-functional. Project Managers coordinate timelines, risk, and communication; Product Managers define outcomes and prioritize value; Developers implement and test; QA/testing validates acceptance criteria and critical flows; and stakeholders provide ongoing feedback and approvals. Communication is treated as a core discipline with regular status updates, milestone reporting, and a documented single source of truth.

Quality assurance and release discipline are integrated throughout delivery. OctoAcme expects unit tests for new logic, integration or smoke testing where needed, CI checks (including security scanning), and manual QA for feature acceptance when appropriate. Before deployment, teams confirm acceptance criteria and CI status, document release notes and rollback plans, verify post-deploy health, and then capture learnings in retrospectives with owned action items for continuous improvement.
